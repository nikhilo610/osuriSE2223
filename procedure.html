    • Procedure Steps for Research Experiment

    • Take pictures of the chair in all four views (top, side, front, orthographic)
    • Record measurements of all parts of the chair
    • Create the bottom base portion (30" x 3.50")
    • Create the angled vertical portion (39" x 3.50")
    • 0.635 millimeter diameter for each hole
    • Create the angled hypotenuse piece

    • Pieces of Fir wood were cut to size using a Delta Vertical Band Saw
    • A Delta Disc and Belt sander was used to sand off the angled portion for the vertical piece
    • Holes screwed in the wood pieces were indented with awls
    • Drive guide used to drill screws in
    • Metal zip ties wrapped around chair legs
    • Wire cutters used to cut off excess metal

    • 50 pound sandbags were placed on the chair to mimic human weight
    • Control Group: Trials run without the apparatus attached
    • Experimental Group: Trials run with the apparatus attached
    • Vernier double range force sensor used to record values
    • Force sensor measured the required force to push the weighted chair over
    • Vernier Logger Lite tracks the measurements on a force by time graph
